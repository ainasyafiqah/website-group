ABOUT CORONAVIRUS

The SARS-CoV-2 virus causes Coronavirus Disease (COVID-19)

The majority of those infected with the virus will have mild to moderate respiratory symptoms and will recover without the need for medical attention. Some, on the other hand, will become critically unwell and require medical assistance. Serious sickness is more likely to strike the elderly and those with underlying medical disorders such as cardiovascular disease, diabetes, chronic respiratory disease, or cancer. COVID-19 can make anyone sick and cause them to get very ill or die without any age limit.

Being thoroughly informed on the disease and how it spreads is the best way to avoid and slow down transmission is by staying at least 1 meter away from others, wear a well fitted mask, and wash your hands or use an alcohol-based rub periodically (hand sanitizer) to protect yourself and others from infection. When it's your turn, get vaccinated and it is advisable to follow the local advice.

When an infected person coughs, sneezes, speaks, sings, or breathes, the virus spreads in microscopic liquid particles from their mouth or nose. Larger respiratory droplets to smaller aerosols are among the particles. If you are sick, it is critical to adopt respiratory etiquette, such as coughing into a flexed elbow, and to stay at home and self-isolate until you heal.
